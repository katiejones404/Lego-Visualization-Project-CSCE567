Goal: Examine Racial Diversity Trends in Lego Minifigures

Welcome to My Project

The goal of this project is to examine trends in racial diversity of Lego over time. Findings are discussed by each graph. Above is the database schema for this project, if you click the image you will see the source data.

Line Graph Notes

This line graph tracks the yearly counts of LEGO minifigure head colors from 1989 to the present day. Historically, LEGO used only “yellow” minifigure heads. This visualization clearly shows this dominance through the early 2000s. Around 2004–2006, there is a sharp rise in realistic skin tones—light, medium, and dark—corresponding with LEGO’s shift toward licensed themes (Harry Potter, Star Wars, etc.) that required character-accurate skincolors. In the 2010s and especially the 2020s, light and medium skin tones increase steadily, while dark tones grow more gradually but consistently. Non-human colors (e.g., blue, green) rise when sci-fi and fantasy themes expand.

Key Finding: Diversity in skin tones has significantly increased since the mid-2000s, however lighter skin tones dominant the frequency of medium/dark skin colors.

Pi Chart Notes

This pie chart highlights which LEGO themes contribute the largest number of darker-tone minifigure heads. Star Wars dominates with over 600 such heads—likely because of its enormous catalog of characters and sets. Friends, Minecraft, and superhero themes also make major contributions.

Key Finding: Themes with large world-building content (Star Wars, Friends) and modern franchises tend to drive the most representation of darker skin tone.

It is important to note that Minecraft characters often use darker skin tones that do not necessarily represent a character’s race. These colors reflect the visual style of the Minecraft game rather than real-world skin tones.

Proportion Chart Notes

This chart can be filtered by Theme Name and Skin Tone Group. Add/remove theme names to see the proportion of different skin tone groups and add/remove skin tone groups to see how Non-Human, Yellow, Dark, Medium, and Light skin tone groups compare across themes.

This chart shows Lego themes and the percentage breakdown of skin-tone categories within that theme. The bars reveal that some themes such as Friends, Super Heroes Marvel/DC, and Star Wars, feature noticeably higher representation of medium and dark skin tones, while older or niche themes still skew heavily light-toned.

Key Finding: Diversity is uneven across themes. Some lines demonstrate deliberate inclusion (due to licensed character's skin tones) while others remain closer to lighter color distributions.

Stacked Bar Notes

This visualization compares traditional minifigures with Lego's “minidoll” figures (used primarily in LEGO Friends and Disney lines). Minidolls overwhelmingly use light skin tones, with medium tones present but rare, and dark tones almost nonexistent. Minifigures, in contrast, show a broader range, though light tones still dominate.

Key Finding:Minidolls remain less diverse than minifigures. This is especially noteworthy because the Friends line, which uses minidolls, is marketed for inclusive play, yet its skin tone distribution is similar and worse than Lego's figure type.

Line Graph for Lego Friends Chart Notes

This chart focuses solely on the LEGO Friends theme, beginning with its launch of sets in 2012. Light skin tones dominate for the first decade. However, around 2023, there is a dramatic rise in darker and non-human tones. This jump corresponds with the widely publicized 2023 Friends reboot, which emphasized diversity and inclusivity.

Key Finding: Friends theme shows a recent, intentional redesign featuring more diverse skin tones. This is evidence of Lego's evolving approach to diversity in its sets.
